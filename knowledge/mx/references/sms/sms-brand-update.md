# SMS: Brand Update

Brand Update is the SMS for sharing a personality moment or a non-promotional brand beat: an anniversary, a small milestone, a stance, a "we're still doing things our way" note. Use it when the goal is to reinforce who the brand is and keep the audience emotionally connected without pushing an offer. Use it when the brand has a real, specific thing to say. Do not use it as a vague "checking in" message, do not use it to backfill a calendar gap, and do not bury an offer inside what is supposed to be a brand beat (use Flash Sale or Event Promo for that). Playbook rule: one clear thought is enough. Personality over detail density. Direct and compact.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: state the thought. Brand name plus one short line of the brand's POV.
- Body: optional one-line extension that sharpens the thought. Skip if the opener stands.
- CTA: one. Soft CTA is acceptable here ("See our story", "Read more", "Look inside"). Shopping verb only if the brief explicitly carries a destination that fits.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the single thought this message lands>

**Product Focus**: <the brand topic, milestone, or stance the message is about>

**Offer**: <typically "No offer">

**Links**: <single shortened destination, often a story or about page>

**Message Type**: Brand Update
```

The brief carries the milestone, the stance, or the update verbatim. The copywriter does not invent founder quotes, dates, or claims about the brand.

## Example structural fragment

```
<BRAND>: <ONE-LINE BRAND THOUGHT FROM BRIEF>. <CTA VERB>: [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message starts pitching a product or sneaking in a discount code, the type has drifted. Strip back to the thought.
