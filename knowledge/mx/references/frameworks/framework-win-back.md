# Framework: Win-Back

Win-Back is the text framework for the winback flow and almost nothing else. Use it inside the winback flow (Email 1 introduces the offer, Email 4 closes with urgency) and for occasional lapsed-customer campaigns when the brand has a real new reason to re-engage. Do not use it for general acquisition, browse abandon, or cart abandon; the framing wrong for those audiences. Do not use it as a sentimental "we miss you" send. Playbook rule: do not sound accusatory or needy. "We miss you" framing is FORBIDDEN; these customers ignored months of campaigns and sentiment does not work on them. The OFFER is the lever, not feelings. Return reason is concrete (store credit waiting, new collection that solves a real gap, mechanic changed). Reactivation should feel like an easy restart, not a guilt trip.

## Structural rules

- Opening: acknowledge the gap without guilt. Short. One beat. "It has been a minute" / "Looking through our records" / "Quick note for you" all work. "We miss you" does not.
- Reason to return: concrete. Store credit with a random dollar amount (e.g. $7.14, not $10.00) is the consistently best-performing winback lever; round numbers tank the email. Alternatives: a new mechanic (BOGO replacing percent), a new collection that resolves a known gap, free gift threshold.
- Reframe for the reader's current state: optional. One line acknowledging that their situation may have changed.
- CTA: one. Destination matches the offer (storefront for credit, specific collection for new drop). Verb is direct ("Use my credit", "See what is new", "Restart my routine").
- Sign-off: optional. Keep light.
- Do not stack incentives. One concrete reason to return is stronger than three weak ones.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the concrete return reason>

**Offer**: <exact mechanic, e.g. "$7.14 store credit applied to account, no code", or "20% off, code WELCOMEBACK, expires <date from brief>">

**Links**: <single verified destination>

**Framework**: Win-Back
```

The brief carries the exact dollar amount or mechanic. The copywriter does not pick "a round number that sounds nice"; round numbers underperform.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: acknowledge the gap without guilt, no "we miss you">

<CONCRETE RETURN REASON: store credit with random amount, new mechanic, or new drop>

<OPTIONAL REFRAME: one line on why now may be different>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email opens with "We noticed you have been away and we just wanted to say...", the framework has drifted into sentiment. Replace with the concrete lever.
