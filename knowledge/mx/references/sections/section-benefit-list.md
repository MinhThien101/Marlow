# Benefit List Section

A Benefit List translates product features into reasons the reader cares. Use it when the product's value depends on several distinct outcomes that work together (ingredients to avoid, guarantees, capabilities). Do not use it as a feature dump. Do not use it when the reader needs explanation, not scanning; that is Educational Content or Feature Highlight. A benefit list is a scan-first section; the reader runs their eyes down the list and lands on the one outcome that matters to them.

## Structural rules

- 3 to 6 items. Below 3 reads thin; above 6 stops scanning.
- Each item: a short label plus a one-line outcome statement.
- Each outcome is an outcome, not a feature. "60-day satisfaction promise" is fine if the value to the reader is obvious; "made with X material" is not.
- Items are concrete and distinct. No two items restate the same outcome with different words.
- Optional short heading and optional one-line setup above the list. No setup paragraph.
- Optional CTA at the bottom. The list can also feed into the next section; not every benefit list needs its own CTA.
- Counts as a SHORT / scan-first section in the structural weight model.

## Placeholder shape in the brief

```
**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Benefit List
3. <next canonical section label>

**Product Focus**: <product, collection, or program the benefits describe>
```

The brief names the section. It does not list the individual benefits. The copywriter pulls those from brand state and verified product facts.

## Example structural fragment

```
## <OPTIONAL HEADING: 2-5 words>

<OPTIONAL SETUP LINE: one sentence, only when needed>

- **<BENEFIT LABEL>:** <ONE OUTCOME LINE>
- **<BENEFIT LABEL>:** <ONE OUTCOME LINE>
- **<BENEFIT LABEL>:** <ONE OUTCOME LINE>
- **<BENEFIT LABEL>:** <ONE OUTCOME LINE>

[<OPTIONAL CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)
```

Bulleted list is the default. A two-column "WHAT / WHY" table is the structured variant when each benefit needs a paired explanation. Either shape is fine; pick one per section and stay consistent.
