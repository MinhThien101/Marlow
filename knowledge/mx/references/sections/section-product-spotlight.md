# Product Spotlight Section

A Product Spotlight is deep focus on a single product without turning into a landing page. Use it when one product is the campaign's reason to exist and the reader needs more than a grid angle to act. Do not use it when several products share the spotlight; that is a Product Grid. Do not use it as a way to list every feature; if the section is becoming exhaustive, the brief is asking for the wrong section.

## Structural rules

- One product, one angle. The angle is the reason this product matters in this send.
- Required: one section heading or short framing line that names the angle.
- Required: 2 to 4 supporting details, each a single short line or bullet.
- Required: one CTA pointing at the spotlighted product.
- The details are concrete (materials, build, behavior, outcome). Generic praise does not count as a detail.
- Length: tight enough that the reader can absorb the angle in one pass. If a feature does not strengthen the named angle, cut it.
- Counts as a HEAVY section. Pair it with scan-first sections after, not with another heavy section in a 3-section email.

## Placeholder shape in the brief

```
**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Product Spotlight
3. <next canonical section label>

**Product Focus**: <single product name, with link in Links field>
```

The brief names the spotlighted product. It does not pick the angle or pre-write the details.

## Example structural fragment

```
## <ANGLE HEADING: 3-6 words naming the angle, not the product name>

<OPTIONAL SUPPORT LINE: one sentence sharpening the angle>

**The Details:**

- **<DETAIL LABEL>:** <ONE CONCRETE SENTENCE supporting the angle>
- **<DETAIL LABEL>:** <ONE CONCRETE SENTENCE supporting the angle>
- **<DETAIL LABEL>:** <ONE CONCRETE SENTENCE supporting the angle>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: shop / claim / build / pick this product>](<verified product link from brief>)
```

Two to four details is the working range. One detail is too thin to justify spotlighting. Five or more reads as a spec sheet; pick the few that carry the angle and let the rest go.
