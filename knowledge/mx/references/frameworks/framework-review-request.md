# Framework: Review Request

Review Request is the text framework for review-request flows. Use it in the review-request flow (typically Email 2, 7 days after Email 1 if no review) and occasional standalone nudges for specific cohorts. Use it only after customers have time to use the product (default: Placed Order + 14 days). Do not send earlier. Do not use it for cross-sells. Playbook rule: ONE ask per email. No cross-sells, social follows, UGC asks, newsletter asks, or product recs. The ask IS the email. Default to no incentive unless the brand confirms platform policy allows it. Light humanity beats heavy persuasion.

## Structural rules

- Opening: short. Gentle check-in. "I know your inbox is crowded" / "Quick ask" / "Hope you have settled in with your [PRODUCT]" all work.
- Review ask: explicit. Direct question or direct ask. "What do you think of your [PRODUCT]?" / "Would you leave a quick review?"
- Why the feedback matters: one short beat. Honest reason: the team reads every review, the brand grows from real feedback, future customers rely on it.
- CTA: one. Deep-link to the specific order's review page when the brief carries it. Verb is direct ("Leave a review", "Share your thoughts").
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice fits well here. Light is right.
- Do not stack a second CTA. No "and while you are there, here is 10% off another product."
- Do not promise an incentive unless the brief explicitly carries one and confirms platform policy.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the review ask>

**Product Focus**: <the specific product the review is for>

**Links**: <verified deep-link to the order's review platform page>

**Framework**: Review Request
```

The brief must carry the deep-link. If it is broken or missing, mark `missing info: <verified review link>` in the brief; do not invent a URL.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: gentle check-in, one short paragraph>

<REVIEW ASK: explicit, one direct question or ask>

<WHY THE FEEDBACK MATTERS: one short honest beat>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: e.g. "Leave a review">](<verified deep-link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email adds a second CTA or a cross-sell, it has drifted. One ask per email. The ask is the email.
