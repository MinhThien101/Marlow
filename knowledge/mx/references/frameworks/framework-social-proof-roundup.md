# Framework: Social Proof Roundup

Social Proof Roundup turns aggregate response into buying confidence. Use it when the brand has real proof: bestseller roundups, "most customers picked," review-quote compilations, or topline metrics (units shipped, customers served, average rating). Use it in welcome flows after the offer and standalone campaigns where proof is the lever. Do not use it without real proof. Do not paraphrase or invent customer language. Playbook rule: real proof only; no vague "everyone loves this." Keep proof scannable and let evidence persuade.

## Structural rules

- Setup line: one short beat. The proof angle: bestsellers, top reviews, milestone, customer count, rating average.
- Proof source or topline result: one explicit number or named source. "Over [COUNT] customers..." / "Our top-rated [CATEGORY]..." / "[COUNT] five-star reviews this year..."
- Top items, categories, or quotes: scannable list or short paragraph. 3 to 5 items if listing. Real product names from the brief.
- Why that matters: one short beat tying the proof to the reader's decision. Skip if the proof carries it.
- CTA: one. Destination is the proof-backed product, collection, or review page. Verb matches ("See the bestsellers", "Read the reviews", "Shop the top picks").
- Sign-off: optional.
- Do not invent customer quotes, review counts, or rating averages.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the proof and the lever it pulls>

**Product Focus**: <the products or category the proof surfaces>

**Required Language**: <exact metrics or quotes if the user provided them, else omit>

**Links**: <verified destination>

**Framework**: Social Proof Roundup
```

The brief must carry verified metrics or approved customer quotes. Unverifiable proof becomes `missing info: <what is missing>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<SETUP: one short beat naming the proof angle>

<PROOF SOURCE OR TOPLINE: one explicit number or named source>

<TOP ITEMS OR QUOTES: scannable list of 3-5 with real product names from brief>

<OPTIONAL "WHY IT MATTERS": one short beat>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email says "customers love this product, you will too," it has drifted into adjectives. Replace with actual review counts, quotes, or top-seller lists.
