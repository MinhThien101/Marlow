# FAQ Section

FAQ is a tight question-and-answer block that resolves real objections. Use it when the reader has 3 to 5 specific friction points the rest of the email cannot address efficiently: safety, comparison to a default option, what-if-it-goes-wrong, how-it-works edge cases. Use it in welcome flows once the offer and brand story are established and the reader is comparing seriously. Do not use it as a dumping ground for product trivia. Do not use it to answer questions the reader is not actually asking. A weak FAQ is invented; a strong FAQ comes from real customer language. Counts as a HEAVY section.

## Structural rules

- 3 to 5 questions. Fewer than 3 is not an FAQ; more than 5 reads as a help-center page.
- Each question is a real objection or a real point of confusion. Not "Q: Is this product great? A: Yes."
- Each answer is direct. Lead with the answer, then add the one supporting detail that matters. Two sentences at most for most answers; three when the topic genuinely needs it.
- Bold the question, bold the start of the answer (e.g. "**A: Yes.**" or "**A: It works differently.**"). The reader is scanning for the verdict first.
- Optional short heading above the questions. Optional one-line setup. No setup paragraph.
- Optional CTA at the bottom pointing at a deeper FAQ page or at the product the answers justify.

## Placeholder shape in the brief

```
**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. FAQ
3. <next canonical section label>

**Product Focus**: <the product or category the questions are about>
```

The brief names the section. It does not pre-write the questions. The copywriter pulls real objections from brand memories, recent reviews, and verified product facts.

## Example structural fragment

```
## <HEADING: signals the topic, e.g. "THE HONEST TRUTH ABOUT X">

**Q: <REAL OBJECTION 1>**
**A: <DIRECT ANSWER STARTING WORD.>** <ONE OR TWO SHORT SENTENCES with the supporting detail that matters.>

**Q: <REAL OBJECTION 2>**
**A: <DIRECT ANSWER STARTING WORD.>** <ONE OR TWO SHORT SENTENCES.>

**Q: <REAL OBJECTION 3>**
**A: <DIRECT ANSWER STARTING WORD.>** <ONE OR TWO SHORT SENTENCES.>

[<OPTIONAL CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)
```

Three questions is the floor and the most common shape. Five is the ceiling. If a sixth feels necessary, the section is doing two jobs; split it or cut the weakest question.
