# Framework: Thank You Note

Thank You Note is the standard post-purchase Email 1 voice. Use it inside the post-purchase flow as the founder thank-you (variants by purchase count: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4+) and for occasional season-specific thank-you sends (year-end, holiday, anniversary, customer-appreciation moments). Use it when sentiment is the entire job and conversion is secondary. Do not use it as a disguised hard sell; if the offer is the lever, use Discount Push. Playbook rule: specific gratitude over generic gratitude. CTA stays SOFT or is omitted. The thank-you should not become a hard sell. The P.S. cross-sell on post-purchase founder-voice thank-yous is allowed and high-leverage, but it is a P.S., not the body.

## Structural rules

- Opening: warm. "Hey [FIRST_NAME]" plus an immediate founder or team voice signal ("It is [FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME]") often.
- Specific reason for appreciation: one or two short beats. Specific moment, specific customer behavior, specific season. Not "thanks for being a customer."
- Small brand or seasonal reflection: one short paragraph tying the thank-you to a real moment or mission beat.
- Soft CTA or omission: optional. If included, the verb is gentle ("See the bestsellers", "Read our story"). Skip if the email is sentiment-only.
- Sign-off: personal. Founder first name common.
- Optional P.S. cross-sell: allowed on post-purchase thank-yous. One product, one link, one short line. Keep separate from the body.
- Do not stack CTAs. Do not turn the email into a roundup.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the gratitude moment and the specific reason>

**Product Focus**: <the product or category the thank-you ties to, or "No product tie-in">

**Offer**: <usually "No offer">

**Framework**: Thank You Note
```

The brief carries the specific moment. The copywriter does not invent a season, a milestone, or a customer behavior.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPTIONAL FOUNDER VOICE LINE: "It is [FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME]">

<SPECIFIC GRATITUDE: one or two short beats with a specific reason, not generic>

<BRAND OR SEASONAL REFLECTION: one short paragraph tying the thank-you to a real moment>

<OPTIONAL SOFT CTA OR OMIT>

<SIGN-OFF: founder first name>

<OPTIONAL P.S. CROSS-SELL: one product, one link, one short line, post-purchase only>

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as "thank you for being a valued customer", the framework has drifted into generic. Replace with the specific moment, the specific customer behavior, or the specific season.
