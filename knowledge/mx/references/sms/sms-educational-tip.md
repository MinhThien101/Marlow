# SMS: Educational Tip

Educational Tip is the SMS for delivering ONE useful idea that quietly reinforces the product's case. Use it when the brand has a concrete claim, a category myth to bust, or a small piece of know-how that lands in one beat. Use it when the lesson naturally connects to the product without forcing a sales pitch. Do not use it as a thin pretext for a discount blast, and do not try to teach more than one thing in a single SMS; SMS is the wrong place to densify. Playbook rule: one lesson only. Teaching concise, not dense. CTA carries reader deeper.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: state the lesson directly. The claim, the fact, the contrast.
- Body: optional one-line product tie-in. Skip if the lesson and the brand are already connected by context.
- CTA: one. Verb pointing deeper ("Learn more", "See how", "Shop the line"). One destination.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the single lesson and the click destination>

**Product Focus**: <the product or line the lesson connects to>

**Offer**: <typically "No offer"; include only if a real offer applies>

**Links**: <single shortened destination, often a PDP or ingredient/spec page>

**Message Type**: Educational Tip
```

The brief carries the verified claim or fact. The copywriter does not invent ingredient claims, certifications, or category facts.

## Example structural fragment

```
<ONE-LINE LESSON OR CLAIM>. <OPTIONAL PRODUCT TIE-IN>. [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message starts piling up multiple claims or trying to cover the whole ingredient list, the type has drifted. Pick one lesson.
