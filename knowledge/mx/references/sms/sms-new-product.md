# SMS: New Product

New Product is the SMS for announcing a launch, drop, or first-time-available item. Use it when the brand has a real new SKU, collection, or variant to put in front of subscribers and the goal is a direct click to the product or learn-more page. Use it when the product can carry the message on its own; the news is the news. Do not use it when there is no actual new product (a fresh photo or a re-merchandised category is not a launch) and do not use it as a thinly veiled bestseller blast. Playbook rule: lead with the product, not the brand backstory. Two or three vivid product traits are enough. End with one direct link CTA.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: name the product immediately. No "we are so excited to share" preamble. No greeting.
- Body: 2-3 short product traits, comma-separated or chained in short sentences. Material, fit, function, feel; whichever lands the desire fastest. Skip if the product name already carries the picture.
- CTA: one. Direct verb plus a shortened link. "Grab yours", "Shop", "See it" all work. One destination.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the launch and the click destination>

**Product Focus**: <the new product name, the canonical traits the copy may name>

**Offer**: <only if the launch carries an offer; otherwise "No offer">

**Links**: <single shortened product or collection URL>

**Message Type**: New Product
```

The brief carries the product name and the two or three traits the copy may use. The copywriter does not invent material claims, fit details, or feature lines.

## Example structural fragment

```
The <PRODUCT_NAME> is here. <TRAIT 1>. <TRAIT 2>. <TRAIT 3>. <CTA VERB>: [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message opens with a sentence about the brand's mission or history before the product name, the type has drifted. Lead with the product.
