# Framework: VIP Invitation

VIP Invitation extends an access-based offer to a subset of the list. Use it inside the VIP flow (Email 1 fires when a customer crosses the spend or order-count threshold) and for occasional VIP-only sends that frame the offer as exclusive: early access drops, members-only bundles, SMS-list invitations for top customers, founder's-circle invitations. Use it when the access genuinely IS different from what the general list sees. Do not use it when the "VIP" offer is identical to the regular promo; the label hollows out and the next VIP send underperforms. Playbook rule: the privilege is concrete (specific dollar threshold, specific access window, specific product allocation). Exclusivity is useful, not fake. CTA is framed as ACCESS, not generic shopping. Generic flash-sale framing is forbidden.

## Structural rules

- Opening: one beat establishing why this person specifically. Implicit ("you have been with us a while") or explicit ("you have crossed [THRESHOLD]"). Short.
- What access means: explicit. The actual privilege: early window, members-only bundle, free gift, allocation guarantee, SMS-only deals, founder's-circle direct line.
- Why it matters: one short beat. The differentiator versus the general list. Skip if the access carries it.
- CTA: one. Framed as access ("Get VIP access", "Claim my early window", "Join the founder's circle"). Not "Shop now".
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common.
- Do not stack privileges. One concrete access beat per send.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the access and why this audience>

**Offer**: <exact privilege mechanic, e.g. "48-hour early access to spring drop", "free gift on orders over [AMOUNT]">

**Links**: <verified destination for the access>

**Framework**: VIP Invitation
```

The brief carries the exact privilege terms. The copywriter does not invent a threshold, a window, or an allocation.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: one beat establishing why this person specifically>

<WHAT ACCESS MEANS: explicit privilege, one short paragraph>

<OPTIONAL DIFFERENTIATOR: one short beat versus the general list>

[<CTA FRAMED AS ACCESS>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads identically to a list-wide promo with "VIP" stamped on top, the framework has drifted. Find the actual differentiator or change the framework.
