# Framework: Discount Push

Discount Push is the most common text framework. Use it when the offer is the whole job: flash sales, last chance, welcome-discount reinforcement, bonus credit, holiday discounts, and urgency closes in abandonment or winback flows. Use it when the reader knows the brand and only needs the offer. Do not use it for first-touch welcome, brand-story sends, or any send where offer is secondary; story or education will bury the math. Playbook rule: lead with the offer, not the brand. Put code/savings near the top. One CTA, one destination. If it needs scene-setting, the angle is wrong.

## Structural rules

- Opening: one short line. Greeting plus a single beat of context, or a flat statement of the offer. No paragraph of setup.
- Offer statement: explicit. The exact discount mechanic (percent, dollar amount, BOGO, free gift threshold, store credit) and the code if there is one. Bold the code.
- Reason to care: one short line tying the offer to a real product payoff. Skip if the offer carries itself.
- Urgency line: one tight sentence. Real urgency only (expiration date, low stock, end of window). Do not invent.
- CTA: one. Verb matches the offer ("Shop the sale", "Claim my discount", "Use my code"). One destination.
- Sign-off: optional. Short. Founder name only if the brand uses founder voice on this send.
- Do not stack a second CTA, a P.S. cross-sell, or a "while you're at it" footer. One offer, one click.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one-sentence goal naming the offer as the campaign job>

**Offer**: <exact mechanic, code, expiration, auto-apply behavior, or "No offer">

**Links**: <single verified destination matching the offer>

**Framework**: Discount Push
```

The brief must carry offer terms verbatim. The copywriter does not invent percentage, code, or expiration. Missing terms become `missing info: <what is missing>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING LINE: one beat of context or a flat offer statement>

For the next <DURATION FROM BRIEF>, <EXACT OFFER MECHANIC FROM BRIEF>.

Use code: **<EXACT CODE FROM BRIEF>**

<OPTIONAL ONE-LINE REASON TO CARE: product payoff>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: matches offer>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If story or brand history creeps above the offer, the framework has drifted. Cut back to the math.
