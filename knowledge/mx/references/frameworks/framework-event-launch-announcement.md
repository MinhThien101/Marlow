# Framework: Event/Launch Announcement

Event/Launch Announcement is for the moment a new product, collection, restock, or event hits. Use it when the news IS the campaign: a product drop, a collection launch, a back-in-stock alert with timing, a live event, a content drop with a hard release window. Use it for waitlist sends and bookmark sends in the days before a drop. Do not use it for ongoing collections or for a generic "shop our store" send. Do not use it as a backdoor discount push; if the offer is the lever, use Discount Push. Playbook rule: lead with the drop, not the background context. The "what is new" list is skimmable, not narrated. The CTA is framed as ACCESS ("See the collection", "Bookmark the page", "Get on the list"), not generic shopping.

## Structural rules

- Opening: one short line stating the news. Time anchor included when relevant ("In 48 hours", "This Friday", "Today").
- What is launching: explicit. Product name, collection name, or event name. If multiple items, list them skimmably (line per item or short bullet list).
- Why it matters: one or two short beats. Concrete differentiators, not adjective stacks. Skip if the news carries itself.
- Timing cue: explicit. Date, window, or scarcity beat. Real timing only; do not invent urgency.
- CTA: one. Framed as access ("Bookmark the page", "See the drop", "Get notified"). Pre-launch sends use waitlist or bookmark CTAs; live-launch sends use shop CTAs.
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common on launch sends.
- Do not bury the news under a brand story or a thank-you preamble.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the drop and the timing>

**Product Focus**: <product, collection, or event being launched>

**Links**: <verified destination, often the collection or waitlist page>

**Required Language**: <exact product names or required dates if the user provided them>

**Framework**: Event/Launch Announcement
```

The brief carries exact product names and timing. The copywriter does not invent a launch date, a price, or a feature.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: time anchor plus the news, e.g. "In 48 hours, [PRODUCT_NAME] lands">

<WHAT IS LAUNCHING: explicit product or collection name, optional one-line description>

<WHY IT MATTERS: one or two short concrete beats, skippable if the news carries itself>

<TIMING CUE: real date, window, or scarcity beat>

[<CTA FRAMED AS ACCESS>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as "we are excited to share that soon we will be launching...", the framework has drifted into preamble. Cut to the news.
