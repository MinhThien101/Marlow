# Framework: Welcome Introduction

Welcome Introduction is the front-door framework. Use it for the warm-introduction text positions in welcome flows (typically Email 2 brand-story or a soft text follow-up) and for any first-touch send where orientation matters more than conversion. Use it when the reader is new and the brand wants to set tone and expectations before pushing. Do not use it for Email 1 of a welcome flow where the discount and CTA must lead (use Discount Push or the DESIGNED welcome template). Do not use it as a hard sell. Playbook rule: this feels like the front door, not a sales page. Orientation and warmth matter. First-touch pressure stays low unless the brief explicitly says otherwise. The product mention is soft; the brand is the headline.

## Structural rules

- Greeting: warm. "Hey [FIRST_NAME]" is the default.
- Welcome moment: one short paragraph. Genuine, not effusive. "Thanks for trusting us" / "Welcome to the [BRAND] community" / "Stoked to have you here" all work.
- Who the brand is: one or two short beats. What the brand stands for in plain language. Not a mission statement.
- What to expect: one short beat. What kind of emails are coming, how often, what the reader gains.
- Soft CTA: one. Optional on sentiment-heavy welcomes. Verb is gentle ("Explore the collection", "Read our story", "See what people are loving"). Not "Shop now".
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice fits well here.
- Do not include a discount unless the brief explicitly carries one as a welcome-discount reinforcement.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the welcome moment and the orientation it provides>

**Offer**: <welcome discount reinforcement only if briefed, else "No offer">

**Links**: <verified destination, often the storefront or a story page>

**Framework**: Welcome Introduction
```

The brief names the welcome moment. The copywriter does not invent founding-story specifics, year numbers, or founder names.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<WELCOME MOMENT: one short paragraph, genuine not effusive>

<WHO THE BRAND IS: one or two short beats in plain language>

<WHAT TO EXPECT: one short beat on cadence and what the reader gains>

[<OPTIONAL SOFT CTA>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as "and right now you can save 30% with code WELCOME!", the framework has drifted into Discount Push. Either change the framework or strip the offer back to a soft reinforcement.
