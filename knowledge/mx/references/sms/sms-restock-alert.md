# SMS: Restock Alert

Restock Alert is the SMS for either "back in stock" or "almost gone again". Use it when a real stock event has happened: a returning SKU, a limited replenishment, or a fast-selling item with verifiable low inventory. Use it when the urgency is genuine and the brand can name the product. Do not use it as a synthetic scarcity tactic ("only a few left" with no real low-stock signal), and do not use it for routine merchandising; readers learn to ignore fake urgency fast. Playbook rule: state the stock situation immediately. Short and urgent. No filler.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: state the stock situation in the first beat. "Back in stock", "Almost gone", "Heads up". No greeting, no scene-setting.
- Body: name the product or collection. Optional one-line urgency cue when the brief carries a real expiration ("Selling fast", "Few pairs left").
- CTA: one. Verb tied to the urgency ("Shop before they sell out", "Grab yours", "Get it"). One destination.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the stock event and the click destination>

**Product Focus**: <the restocked or low-stock product or collection name>

**Offer**: <only if a restock offer applies; otherwise "No offer">

**Links**: <single shortened product or collection URL>

**Message Type**: Restock Alert
```

The brief carries the verified stock signal. The copywriter does not invent quantities, units left, or expiration windows.

## Example structural fragment

```
<STOCK SIGNAL>. <PRODUCT OR COLLECTION NAME> is <STATE>. <CTA VERB>: [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message opens with a brand update or a feature explanation before the stock signal, the type has drifted. Lead with the alert.
