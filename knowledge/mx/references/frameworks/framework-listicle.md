# Framework: Listicle

Listicle organizes value into clear digestible units. Use it when the campaign covers a set of distinct items the reader benefits from scanning: top picks, essentials roundup, a numbered set of reasons, a curated category lineup, a "five ways to use this" guide. Use it in standalone campaigns when scannability is the lever. Do not use it when one product deserves the full focus (use Product Spotlight section or Discount Push framework). Do not overstuff. Playbook rule: the list makes choice EASIER, not noisier. Each item is distinct. Do not stuff the email with 10 bullets when 5 would land harder.

## Structural rules

- Setup line: one short beat naming what the list covers and why now.
- List: 3 to 6 items. Each item has a name and one short reason to care. Numbered, bulleted, or short paragraph blocks; pick one and hold it.
- Each item distinct: items should not overlap. If two bullets could be merged, merge them.
- CTA: one. Destination matches the list's job (collection page, category page, shop-all). Verb is direct ("Shop the essentials", "See the picks", "Explore the lineup").
- Sign-off: optional. Light.
- Do not include a 7th item to make the list look thorough. Five strong items beat seven weak ones.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the list and the lever>

**Product Focus**: <the products, categories, or themes in the list>

**Required Language**: <exact product names from the brief>

**Links**: <verified destination>

**Framework**: Listicle
```

The brief carries the exact product names. The copywriter does not invent items to fill a count.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<SETUP: one short beat naming the list and the timing>

- **<ITEM 1 NAME>**: <one short distinct reason>
- **<ITEM 2 NAME>**: <one short distinct reason>
- **<ITEM 3 NAME>**: <one short distinct reason>
- **<ITEM 4 NAME>**: <one short distinct reason>
- **<ITEM 5 NAME>**: <one short distinct reason>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If two items rhyme or restate each other, the list has drifted into padding. Cut to the items that are genuinely distinct.
