# Framework: Order Update

Order Update is the operational text framework for transactional and account-status sends. Use it when customers need clear facts about an order, subscription, or delivery: shipment confirmation, cancellation confirmation, payment update, delay notice, return processed notice. Use it when clarity matters more than persuasion. Do not disguise sales as operational updates; upsell pivots read as manipulative. Playbook rule: keep it operationally clear. Do not turn a simple update into a pitch. If there is a win-back angle (cancelled/paused), keep it secondary at most.

## Structural rules

- Opening: factual. The change, the status, or the event stated plainly. "Your order has shipped." / "Your subscription is now cancelled." / "Your refund has been processed."
- What it means: one short beat. The practical consequence (no more shipments, estimated delivery date, refund window). Skip if the opening carries it.
- Next step or support path: one short beat. The path forward, the support contact, or the option to undo/change. Real and verifiable.
- Optional soft win-back or re-engagement beat: one short line MAX. Skip on most operational sends; include only when the brief explicitly carries it.
- CTA: one or none. Operational sends often need no CTA. When included, the verb matches the action ("Track my order", "Update my preferences", "Contact support"). Do not stack CTAs.
- Sign-off: optional. Light or omitted.
- Do not invent a tracking number, a delivery date, a refund amount, or a policy term.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the operational update>

**Required Language**: <exact policy or status terms from the brief>

**Links**: <verified destination if a CTA is included, e.g. tracking page, account page>

**Framework**: Order Update
```

The brief must carry verified status terms. Unknown specifics become `missing info: <what is missing>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: factual statement of the change, status, or event>

<WHAT IT MEANS: one short beat on the practical consequence>

<NEXT STEP OR SUPPORT PATH: one short beat with real options>

<OPTIONAL SOFT RE-ENGAGEMENT LINE: skip unless brief explicitly carries it>

[<OPTIONAL CTA>]([TRACKING_URL_OR_ACCOUNT_URL])

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email pivots to "here is 20% off your next order," it has drifted into sales. Cut the pivot. Operational stays operational.
