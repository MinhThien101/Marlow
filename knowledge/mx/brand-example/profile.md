# Brand Profile: Spruce Coffee Co.

## Positioning

Specialty coffee roaster built around accessibility. Spruce sells single-origin
and blended coffees to home brewers who want better than supermarket but do not
want to learn a new language to buy a bag. The brand sits between commodity
grocery coffee and gear-obsessed specialty roasters.

Founded 2022, Portland, Oregon. Direct-to-consumer only. No wholesale.

Average order value: $24-48. Subscription accounts for roughly 40% of revenue.


## Founder

Mara Ellis. Former barista and coffee shop manager. Left the industry in 2019,
came back in 2022 with a roastery. No formal business background; built the
brand through a mailing list before the site launched. Still writes most of the
brand emails personally.

Mara's presence is felt in the brand voice: warm, plainspoken, willing to admit
when something did not work.


## Product line

4 single-origin coffees ($18-24 each): sourced seasonally, rotated 2-3 times
per year. Hero attributes are origin story and traceability.

3 blends ($16-20 each): designed for everyday use. Consistent flavor profile
across roast seasons. The "Morning Standard" blend is the top seller.

Starter kit ($48): includes one blend, one single-origin, a paper filter
sampler, and a one-page brewing guide written by Mara. Best entry point for new
customers.

Subscription ($32/mo): one bag per month, customer chooses blend or rotating
single-origin track. Subscribers get a 10% discount on add-ons.


## Current offer

Free shipping on orders over $40. No active discount code. Subscription has a
30-day pause option.


## Customer archetype

Age 28-45. Drinks coffee daily, usually at home. Uses a pour-over or French
press. Skeptical of supermarket coffee but has not committed to high-end gear.
Willing to spend more per bag if the story checks out. Reads the emails.


## Brand-wide constraints

- Do not claim health benefits.
- Do not use the word "artisanal" or "craft". Mara finds them overused.
- Do not reference competitors by name.
- Do not invent promotional offers. Use only what is listed in this file.
- Subject lines should not use ALL CAPS words.
- Avoid exclamation points in body copy. One per email maximum, in subject or
  preview only.
