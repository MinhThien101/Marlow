# Product Grid Section

A product grid is the scan-friendly browse block. Use it when the reader's job is to pick from several products, collections, or categories. Each item gets one short angle. Use a grid when no single product needs to dominate. Do not use a grid when one product is the actual hero of the send; promote that one to a Product Spotlight and let the grid go. Do not use a grid as a junk drawer for SKUs the campaign could not place elsewhere.

## Structural rules

- 2 to 6 items. Below 2 it is not a grid; above 6 it stops being scannable.
- Each item: short label (product name, collection, or category) plus one short angle line.
- Angle lines are one short sentence. Concrete. Distinct from the other items.
- One shared CTA at the bottom is the default. Per-item links inside the table cells are also fine; do not stack both.
- Optional short section heading above the table. Optional short setup line under the heading. No setup paragraphs.
- If one item starts needing multiple supporting lines, the section type is wrong, not the item. Move that item to Product Spotlight.
- No item should repeat another item's angle. A grid of near-identical angles reads as filler.

## Placeholder shape in the brief

```
**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Product Grid
3. <next canonical section label>

**Product Focus**: <products, collection, or category the grid covers>
```

The brief names the grid as a section and names which products belong in scope. It does not pre-write the angles. The copywriter picks one angle per item.

## Example structural fragment

```
## <OPTIONAL SECTION HEADING: 2-5 words>

<OPTIONAL SETUP LINE: one sentence, only if the heading needs framing>

| <PRODUCT 1 NAME> | <PRODUCT 2 NAME> |
| --- | --- |
| <ONE SHORT ANGLE: distinct from item 2> | <ONE SHORT ANGLE: distinct from item 1> |

| <PRODUCT 3 NAME> | <PRODUCT 4 NAME> |
| --- | --- |
| <ONE SHORT ANGLE> | <ONE SHORT ANGLE> |

[<SHARED CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)
```

Two columns is the safe default. Three columns work when angles are tight enough to fit. Single column reads as stacked body copy; if you find yourself there, the section is not actually a grid.
