# Site abandon flow

## Education

The lightest-touch abandonment flow. Visitor was on site but did not view a specific product. Intent is lowest of abandonment flows, so keep it short and broad. Aggressive selling here burns the list.

- **Audience.** On site with no specific product viewed. Likely orienting, comparing brands, or exploring.
- **Trigger.** Active on Site. Email 1 fires 1-4 hours after abandonment (3 hours default) on both branches. Faster than 1 hour feels invasive.
- **Strategic purpose.** Give a clear re-entry point and stay top-of-mind without heavy conversion pressure.
- **Core mechanic.** Broad merchandising: bestsellers, top categories, hero collections. Do not pretend product-level interest.
- **Playbook shape.** Exactly 2 emails per branch with mandatory buyer-history split. Past-buyer branch has no discount; new-buyer branch carries the welcome discount in both emails. Do not extend to 3+ emails per branch.
- **TEXT_BASED placement.** Email 2 on each branch (`framework-founder-letter`). Personal check-in voice.
- **What does not belong.** Product-specific dynamic blocks/copy. "Your product is waiting" language. "Order is ready to ship" framing (belongs in cart/checkout abandon). Discounts on past-buyer branch.

## Template

| Branch | # | Title | Type | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trigger | - | Active on Site | METRIC | n/a |
| Past buyer | 1 | Did You Find What You Needed? | DESIGNED | Wait 3 hours, if past buyer |
| Past buyer | 2 | Need Help? Quick Note | TEXT_BASED | Wait 1 day |
| New buyer | 1 | Did You Find It? + Welcome Offer | DESIGNED | Wait 3 hours, if new buyer |
| New buyer | 2 | Need Help? + Welcome Offer | TEXT_BASED | Wait 1 day |

Per-node recommendations:

| Branch / # | Recommended sections / framework | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Past 1 | Hero Section + Product Grid + Support Block | Broad bestsellers / categories. Clear above-the-fold browse CTA. Soft support tone near the end. No discount. ALTERNATIVE: Hero + Lifestyle Content + Product Grid for brands that merchandise by mood. |
| Past 2 | `framework-founder-letter` | Personal check-in voice. "Noticed you stopped by, anything I can help with?" May suggest bestsellers. No discount. |
| New 1 | Hero Section + Discount Offer + Product Grid | Broad merchandising plus welcome discount. ALTERNATIVE: Hero + Product Grid + Promo Banner when discount fits cleanly as a top scan-first reminder. |
| New 2 | `framework-founder-letter` | Personal check-in. Welcome discount reminder in text form (no need for graphic banner). |

Required brand-specific facts (mark `missing info: <what is missing>` if unverifiable):

- Top 1-3 categories or collections to feature in Email 1.
- Bestseller set to highlight.
- Welcome offer code and rules (new-buyer branch only).
- Founder or support voice cues for Email 2.

## Example (Past-buyer Email 1 brief sketch)

```
## Did You Find What You Needed?

**Campaign Direction**: Give the past buyer a broad, helpful re-entry point. No discount, no product-specific framing.

**Product Focus**: <TOP 1-3 BESTSELLERS OR HERO CATEGORIES>

**Offer**: No offer.

**Links**: <VERIFIED CATEGORY OR COLLECTION URL>

**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Product Grid
3. Support Block

**Design Notes**: Clear above-the-fold browse CTA. Product Grid highlights bestsellers or hero categories at a scannable depth. Support Block ends with a soft "questions? reach out" tone. No product-specific dynamic blocks.

**Flow Context**: Site Abandon Flow, past-buyer branch, Email 1 of 2. Fires 3 hours after Active on Site. No discount on this branch in either email.
```

The brief defines broad merchandising targets and no-offer state. The writer pulls bestseller names and headline copy from brand state.
