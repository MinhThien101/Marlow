# Framework: Community Spotlight

Community Spotlight shows the list real customer signal. Use it when you have concrete community proof: recurring language, recent UGC, testimonial roundup, or a "what people are saying" moment. It works in welcome flows (to build belonging) and standalone campaigns (when proof is the lever). Do not use it without real customer language. Playbook rule: spotlight the CUSTOMER, not brand ego. "Community" must be specific, not generic "family" copy. Use real customer language when the brief provides it.

## Structural rules

- Opening: founder or team voice. Short. "It is [FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME], and I wanted to share something" / "Reading through messages this month" all work.
- Community setup: one short paragraph naming the moment and the source (reviews, replies, DMs, UGC, customer service responses).
- Quotes, themes, or observed pattern: explicit. Real customer language when the brief carries it. A pattern observation ("one word kept coming up: finally") when individual quotes are not available. Scannable.
- What it says about the brand or customer: one short beat tying the community signal back to the reader.
- CTA: one. Soft CTA when the email is sentiment-led ("See what others are loving", "Read the reviews"). Skip if the spotlight is sentiment-only.
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common.
- Do not invent customer quotes. Do not paraphrase into "ideal" customer language.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the community moment and the source>

**Required Language**: <exact customer quotes if the user or brand provided them>

**Links**: <verified destination, often a review page or collection>

**Framework**: Community Spotlight
```

The brief must include verified quotes or approved community signal. Missing quotes become `missing info: <approved customer quote>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: founder or team voice, one short personal line>

<COMMUNITY SETUP: one short paragraph naming the moment and source>

<QUOTES OR PATTERN: real customer language or observed pattern, scannable>

<WHAT IT SAYS: one short beat tying the signal to the reader>

[<OPTIONAL SOFT CTA>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email says "our family" or "our tribe" without a real customer beat, it has drifted into brand ego. Replace with an actual quote, pattern, or moment.
