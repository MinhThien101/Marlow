# SMS: Social Proof

Social Proof is the SMS for borrowing credibility from real buyers, reviews, or results. Use it when the brand has approved quote material, a verifiable review count, or a concrete result claim and the goal is to reduce risk quickly. Use it when the proof itself is the news. Do not use it for vague "everyone loves this" language with no specific signal behind it, and do not invent reviewer names, counts, or quotes. Playbook rule: real proof only. Lead with the proof signal, not setup. Avoid vague "customers love this" wording.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: lead with the proof. Quote in actual quote marks, or a real count, or a specific result. No "many of our customers say..." preamble.
- Body: optional one-line product or claim that the proof supports. Skip if the proof carries the message.
- CTA: one. Shopping verb plus a shortened link.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the proof and the click destination>

**Product Focus**: <the product the proof supports>

**Required Language**: <the exact quote or claim verbatim, in quote marks>

**Links**: <single shortened destination matching the product>

**Message Type**: Social Proof
```

The brief carries the exact quote or review count verbatim. The copywriter does not invent reviewer names, counts, or paraphrase quotes.

## Example structural fragment

```
"<EXACT CUSTOMER QUOTE FROM BRIEF>" <REAL COUNT OR DETAIL>. <OPTIONAL PRODUCT CUE>. [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message uses generic praise language without a real quote or number behind it, the type has drifted. Drop the praise and bring back the specific.
