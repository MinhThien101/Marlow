# Framework: Upsell

Upsell makes the next-level option feel logical, not pressured. Use it post-purchase when the customer's first product has had time to land (subscription upgrade, larger pack, bundle add-on, multi-month commitment from a single-month start, accessory that completes the original purchase). Use it inside replenishment flows when subscriptions or larger packs are a natural extension. Do not use it cold; the reader needs an existing relationship with the original product. Do not use it as a discount-led push; that is Discount Push. Playbook rule: anchor the upsell in CONTINUITY, not pressure. Added value is obvious or the upsell fails. The reader should feel helped, not milked. If the upsell only benefits the brand's margin, the framework is the wrong choice.

## Structural rules

- Opening: short. Acknowledge the current product or relationship in one beat.
- Show what the next level unlocks: one short paragraph. Concrete benefits: time horizon, convenience, savings math, fewer reorders, complementary use.
- Justify the bigger commitment: one beat. Why now, why this customer, why this size. Skip if the previous beat carries it.
- CTA: one. Destination is the upsell product or upgrade page. Verb matches ("Upgrade to [SIZE]", "Switch to subscription", "Add the [ACCESSORY]").
- Sign-off: optional.
- Do not stack two upsells. One next-level option per send.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the current product and the specific upsell>

**Product Focus**: <the upsell product or upgrade path>

**Offer**: <subscription savings, bundle discount, or "No incremental offer">

**Links**: <verified destination for the upsell>

**Framework**: Upsell
```

The brief names the current product and the upsell path. The copywriter does not invent a savings percentage or a subscription benefit.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: one beat acknowledging the current product or relationship>

<WHAT THE NEXT LEVEL UNLOCKS: one short paragraph with concrete continuity, convenience, or savings>

<OPTIONAL JUSTIFICATION: one beat on why now>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: matches the upsell>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as "and while you are at it, here is another product", the framework has drifted into cross-sell. Cross-sell belongs in a P.S. on a Founder Letter or in a Product Grid. Upsell is the same product family, one level up.
