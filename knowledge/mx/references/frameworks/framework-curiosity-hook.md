# Framework: Curiosity Hook

Curiosity Hook is the pre-launch tease framework. Use it in the days before a drop, a restock, a collection launch, or an event when the brand wants to seed anticipation without revealing everything. Use it for waitlist sends, "something is coming" sends, and SMS-list signup pushes anchored to a future drop. Do not use it for the actual launch (that is Event/Launch Announcement). Do not use it without a real reveal coming; teasing without delivery breaks trust. Playbook rule: tease enough to intrigue, not so little it feels empty. One or two concrete clues beat vague mystery. Keep the copy tight; the framework loses force at length.

## Structural rules

- Tease: one or two short lines. The reveal vector (a new product, a new flavor, a new collection) without the full details.
- Concrete clue: one or two specifics. Audience cue, use case, vibe anchor, or a single distinguishing feature. Not "a brand new something you will love."
- Timing cue or scarcity note: one short line. Real date, real window, or real scarcity. Do not invent.
- CTA: one. Soft CTAs work well here ("Get notified", "Join the waitlist", "Save the date"). Hard shop CTAs do not fit; the product is not live yet.
- Sign-off: optional. Light.
- Keep total length short. Curiosity diminishes the more you say.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence on the tease and what it points at>

**Product Focus**: <the upcoming drop, even if it stays unnamed in the copy>

**Links**: <verified waitlist or notify-me destination>

**Framework**: Curiosity Hook
```

The brief carries the real reveal date and the concrete clues. The copywriter does not invent a launch date or a feature.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<TEASE: one or two short lines naming the reveal vector>

<CONCRETE CLUE: one or two specifics on audience, use case, or distinguishing feature>

<TIMING CUE: real date or window, one short line>

[<SOFT CTA: e.g. "Get notified">](<verified waitlist link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as fully descriptive ("our new [PRODUCT] launches Friday with these features..."), the framework has drifted into Event/Launch Announcement. Either change the framework or pull back to clues only.
