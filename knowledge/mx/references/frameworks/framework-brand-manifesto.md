# Framework: Brand Manifesto

Brand Manifesto stakes the brand's position out loud. Use it when the brand has a real stance worth declaring: an ingredient principle the category violates, a pricing philosophy that differs from the default, a quality standard the brand will not budge on, a values position tied to product reality. Use it occasionally; manifestos lose force at frequency. Best in welcome flows after the offer and brand story have landed, and in standalone campaigns when the brand needs to recenter on principles. Do not use it without a concrete stance; empty mission language reads as performative. Playbook rule: conviction over cleverness. Abstract values must tie to product reality. Avoid empty mission-language with no concrete stance.

## Structural rules

- Belief statement: one short paragraph. The core conviction stated plainly. "We believe [X]. Period."
- What the brand rejects: one short beat. Concrete. Named practices, named shortcuts, named compromises the brand does not make.
- What the brand stands for: one short beat. Concrete. Named standards, named sourcing, named commitments.
- Product or collection tie-in: one short line connecting the principle to a real product or category page.
- CTA: optional. If included, soft. Verb matches ("See our standards", "Read our ingredient list", "Shop the line that lives by this").
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common; manifestos read stronger when signed.
- Do not stack three values. One conviction stated clearly beats a paragraph of mission talk.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence stating the conviction the email declares>

**Product Focus**: <the product, ingredient list, or process page the conviction ties to>

**Links**: <verified destination that backs the conviction up>

**Framework**: Brand Manifesto
```

The brief carries the verified standards or ingredient list. The copywriter does not invent a sourcing claim or a manufacturing practice.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<BELIEF STATEMENT: one short paragraph, plain conviction>

<WHAT WE REJECT: one short beat with named specifics>

<WHAT WE STAND FOR: one short beat with named specifics>

<PRODUCT TIE-IN: one short line>

[<OPTIONAL SOFT CTA>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as "we are committed to quality and authenticity", the framework has drifted into empty mission talk. Replace with the specific practice the brand will not do and the specific standard the brand will not break.
