# SMS: Loyalty Reward

Loyalty Reward is the SMS for confirming that a subscriber is in a privileged tier and converting that status into a click: early access, exclusive drops, SMS-community-only offers, VIP previews. Use it inside an SMS welcome sequence to set the tone of the channel, or as part of a recurring VIP cadence when the brand has a real, differentiated benefit to offer. Use it when the reward is specific and earned. Do not use it to dress up a general blast as exclusive, and do not promise access the brand will not actually deliver. Playbook rule: reward language specific. Status signal clear. One crisp CTA.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: confirm the status in one beat. "You're in", "Welcome to the list", "VIP access unlocked". Specific over generic.
- Body: name what the reward actually is. Early access, exclusive drops, member-only offers. Skip vague "perks" language.
- CTA: one. Verb tied to the reward ("See what's new", "Shop early", "Claim access"). One destination.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the status confirmation and the first reward action>

**Product Focus**: <the reward content, drop, or access page>

**Offer**: <reward terms, or "Access is the offer">

**Links**: <single shortened destination matching the reward>

**Message Type**: Loyalty Reward
```

The brief carries the status language and the reward content. The copywriter does not invent tiers, point balances, or benefits the brand does not run.

## Example structural fragment

```
<STATUS CONFIRMATION>. <SPECIFIC REWARD: early access, exclusive drops, member-only offers>. <CTA VERB>: [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message uses generic "thanks for being a member" language with no specific reward behind it, the type has drifted. Name the access.
