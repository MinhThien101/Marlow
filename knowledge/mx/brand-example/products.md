# Products: Spruce Coffee Co.

## Single-Origin Coffees

### Huila, Colombia - $22

Origin: Huila department, Colombia. Small lot, farmer cooperative.
Process: washed.
Flavor profile: bright acidity, stone fruit, clean finish.
Hero attribute: full traceability to the cooperative and harvest year.
Common objection: "It's too acidic for me." - Acknowledge and redirect to
  Morning Standard blend.
Stock note: seasonal. Rotates out when the lot is exhausted.


### Oaxaca, Mexico - $20

Origin: Sierra Juarez, Oaxaca.
Process: natural.
Flavor profile: chocolate-forward, low acidity, slight fruit on the back end.
Hero attribute: accessible flavor profile for people moving off dark roast.
Common objection: "Is natural-process weird?" - Explain briefly that natural
  just means the fruit dried on the bean; the result is fuller body, not
  fermented flavor.


### West Java, Indonesia - $24

Origin: Preanger region. Single estate.
Process: wet-hulled (giling basah).
Flavor profile: earthy, herbal, low brightness. Full body.
Hero attribute: unusual process creates a cup that is distinct from Central
  American or African origins.
Common objection: "Earthy sounds like dirt." - Position as bold and grounding
  rather than off-putting. Good for French press.


### Ethiopia Yirgacheffe - $22

Origin: Gedeo Zone, Ethiopia.
Process: natural.
Flavor profile: blueberry, jasmine, wine-like finish.
Hero attribute: the most "distinctive" coffee in the lineup; strong identifier
  for specialty coffee newcomers who want to understand what the fuss is about.
Common objection: "It tastes like fruit, not coffee." - Frame as a feature,
  not a bug. Best for pour-over.


## Blends

### Morning Standard - $16

Blend of Central American and Indonesian components. Designed for consistency
across roast seasons: same flavor profile regardless of what lot is in the
hopper. Spruce's top seller. Entry point for new buyers who want reliability.
Grind recommendation: works in any brewer.


### Easy Dark - $18

Darker roast on the same base as Morning Standard. Lower acidity. Designed for
customers transitioning off supermarket dark roast. Not burned; just roasted
further to reduce brightness.


### Decaf House - $20

Swiss water process decaf. Brazilian base. Chocolate and nut notes.
Positioned honestly: "A good decaf, not a great caffeinated coffee pretending
to be decaf." No caffeine claims beyond standard decaf disclosure.


## Kits and Subscriptions

### Starter Kit - $48

Contents: one Morning Standard (12 oz), one single-origin of the week (current
lot), a pack of Hario V60 filters, and Mara's one-page brewing guide.
Hero attribute: lowers the barrier to trying specialty coffee. One purchase
covers everything needed for a first pour-over.
Common objection: "I don't have a pour-over setup." - Kit does not include
  hardware. Note this clearly. Redirect non-pour-over buyers to the blends.


### Monthly Subscription - $32/mo

One 12 oz bag per month. Customer chooses at signup: Morning Standard every
month, or rotating single-origin track.
Subscribers get 10% off add-on orders.
Pause: 30-day pause available, no fee.
Cancel: anytime.
Objection handling: emphasize the pause option for customers who are traveling
  or overstocked.
