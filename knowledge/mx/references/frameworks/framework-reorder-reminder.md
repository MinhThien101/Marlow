# Framework: Reorder Reminder

Reorder Reminder is the text framework for replenishment flows on consumable products. Use it for supplements, skincare, food, coffee, pet food, cleaning supplies, and similar SKUs that run out on a predictable cycle. Use it inside the replenishment flow (Email 1 fires on expected reorder date) and for occasional standalone sends when the brand has good signal that a customer is about to run out. Do not use it for durable goods (no one reorders a jacket every 30 days). Do not use it as a discount push; the lever is continuity, not savings. Playbook rule: continuity is the emotional lever. Practical, not alarmist ("you ran out" is wrong; "before you run out" is right). Subscriptions or larger packs appear naturally only when the brief explicitly calls for them.

## Structural rules

- Opening: one short line on the running-low moment. Personal, not robotic. "Squeezing the last drop" / "the bottle is getting light" / "the routine is almost out" all work.
- Why continuity matters: one short beat. The cost of running out (skipping a day, breaking the habit, the routine breaking). Skip if the opening carries it.
- Easy reorder path: explicit. One-click reorder language, last-purchased product reference if the brief carries it.
- CTA: one. Destination is the reorder or product page. Verb is direct ("Restock now", "Reorder my [PRODUCT]", "Keep it going").
- Sign-off: optional. Light.
- Do not pile on urgency. The customer is not in a flash-sale moment; they are in a habit-continuity moment.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the running-low moment and the continuity stake>

**Product Focus**: <the consumable being reordered>

**Offer**: <subscription upgrade if the brief calls for it, or "No incremental offer">

**Links**: <verified reorder destination>

**Framework**: Reorder Reminder
```

The brief carries the product name and the reorder URL. The copywriter does not invent a consumption rate or a "you have run out" claim.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: one short personal line on the running-low moment>

<OPTIONAL CONTINUITY BEAT: one short line on the cost of running out>

<EASY REORDER PATH: one line referencing the last-purchased product if briefed>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: direct, e.g. "Restock now">](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email reads as alarmist ("Do not let your skin crash!"), the framework has drifted. Continuity is the lever, not panic.
