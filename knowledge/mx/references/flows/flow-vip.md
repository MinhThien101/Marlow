# VIP flow

## Education

Retention and sentiment, not conversion mechanics. Reward and retain highest-value customers when they hit VIP status. This flow should make them feel like top customers.

- **Audience.** Customer who just hit a brand-defined VIP threshold (spend, order count, or both). Already familiar with brand/products. Does not need education or persuasion.
- **Trigger.** VIP segment defined by brand spend/order threshold. Without a threshold, segment cannot be built.
- **Strategic purpose.** Retention through recognition. Make the customer feel valued and treated differently from the general list.
- **Core mechanic.** Email 1 offer MUST feel meaningfully different from general-list offers. If identical to regular campaigns, VIP label is hollow.
- **Playbook shape.** Exactly 2 emails. Email 1 fires immediately on segment entry. Email 2 lands 5 days later. Keep 5-day spacing so each email has a distinct job.
- **TEXT_BASED placement.** Email 2 (`framework-founder-letter`). Sentiment only. No new offer or product pitch.
- **Voice.** Genuine and personal across both emails. Generic flash-sale framing is forbidden; this flow must feel different.
- **Scope.** Situational, not for every account. Most useful for brands with meaningful repeat-purchase behavior.
- **What does not belong.** Generic flash-sale framing in Email 1. New offer in Email 2. Product pitches in Email 2. Cold/corporate voice.

## Template

| # | Title | Type | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trigger | VIP Threshold Reached | SEGMENT | n/a |
| 1 | Welcome to VIP + Exclusive Offer | DESIGNED | Send immediately |
| 2 | Founder Thank You | TEXT_BASED | Wait 5 days |

Per-node recommendations:

| # | Recommended sections / framework | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hero Section + Discount Offer + Benefit List | Recognize VIP status in the Hero headline. Frame the offer as VIP-only / exclusive in Discount Offer. Benefit List carries "what makes this VIP-only" cues (early access, exclusive products, special treatment). Code prominent. ALTERNATIVE: Hero + Discount Offer + Product Spotlight when the VIP offer is tied to a specific premium product. |
| 2 | `framework-founder-letter` | Real signature line. Genuine thank-you. No new offer. No product pitch. May include a soft "reach out anytime" or "your account manager is X" line if the brand has that level of service. |

Required brand-specific facts (mark `missing info: <what is missing>` if unverifiable):

- The VIP threshold (spend amount, order count, or combination).
- The exclusive offer: what it is, why it is different from public offers, and how long it is good for.
- The founder's voice or signature for Email 2.

## Example (Email 1 brief sketch)

```
## Welcome to VIP + Exclusive Offer

**Campaign Direction**: Recognize the customer's new VIP status and frame the offer as exclusive to VIPs. Must feel meaningfully different from the brand's public offers.

**Product Focus**: <VIP COLLECTION OR PREMIUM PRODUCT IF TIED TO ONE, OTHERWISE NO PRODUCT FOCUS>

**Offer**: <EXACT VIP-ONLY OFFER>, code <EXACT CODE>, valid <DURATION>, eligibility <RULES>

**Links**: <VERIFIED VIP COLLECTION OR CHECKOUT URL>

**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Discount Offer
3. Benefit List

**Design Notes**: Hero headline recognizes VIP status by name. Discount Offer frames the offer as VIP-only / exclusive. Benefit List names what makes the offer different (early access, exclusive product, dollar value, etc.). No generic flash-sale framing.

**Flow Context**: VIP Flow, Email 1 of 2. Fires immediately on segment entry. Email 2 follows 5 days later with a founder thank-you and no new offer.
```

The brief defines offer terms and exclusivity framing. The writer composes headline and benefit cues from brand state.
