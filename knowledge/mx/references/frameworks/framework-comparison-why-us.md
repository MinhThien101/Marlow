# Framework: Comparison / Why Us

Comparison / Why Us makes differentiation concrete by contrasting the default option. Use it when comparison drives purchase: high-markup categories, weak default options, or known competitor reference points. It fits welcome flows after the offer, standalone "us vs them" campaigns, and objection-heavy moments. Do not use it without specifics. Do not strawman alternatives. Playbook rule: be clear and specific. Avoid vague "better quality" claims. The reader should leave with numbers or named differences, not adjectives.

## Structural rules

- Opening: short. Frame the comparison or surface the misconception. "Most people do not realize..." / "There is a quiet markup most people miss..." all work.
- What the usual alternative gets wrong: one short paragraph. Specific. Named markup, named failure mode, named cost.
- What this brand or product does differently: one short paragraph. Concrete mechanism. Real numbers when the brief carries them.
- Optional bulleted contrast: short, scannable, fair. Two or three lines max.
- CTA: one. Destination is a process page, a comparison page, or the product itself. Verb matches ("See the honest truth", "Compare for yourself", "See how it works").
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common when the comparison is a brand-stance moment.
- Do not invent a competitor name, a markup percentage, or a category statistic.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the comparison and the brand's specific difference>

**Product Focus**: <the product or process the comparison points at>

**Links**: <verified destination, often a process or comparison page>

**Framework**: Comparison / Why Us
```

The brief must carry the specifics. Unknown markups or category numbers become `missing info: <what is missing>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: one short line framing the comparison>

<WHAT THE ALTERNATIVE GETS WRONG: one short paragraph with one concrete specific>

<WHAT THIS BRAND DOES DIFFERENTLY: one short paragraph with the concrete mechanism>

<OPTIONAL CONTRAST LINES: two or three bullets, fair not strawman>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the comparison says "we are the best in the industry," it has drifted into adjectives. Replace with a specific number, mechanism, or named difference.
