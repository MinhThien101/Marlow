# SMS: Welcome Offer

Welcome Offer is the SMS that pairs a first-touch hello with the signup discount in a single short message. Use it inside an SMS welcome sequence, typically the very first send after a subscriber joins, or as a follow-up nudge when the welcome email's offer has not been used. Use it when the brand has a real welcome offer to deliver and the goal is one clear first action. Do not use it for general acquisition blasts, do not use it to introduce a non-welcome offer, and do not turn it into a brand-story tour; the welcome is one beat, the offer is the other beat, that is it. Playbook rule: welcome and offer in one tight message. Code not buried. Feels like the first move, not the full story.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: short welcome plus brand name. One sentence. No greeting on its own line.
- Body: state the offer mechanic and the code in plain text. Codes uppercase, no decoration.
- CTA: one. Direct shopping verb plus a shortened link. "Shop now", "Start here", "Use it here" all fit.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the welcome and the first action>

**Offer**: <exact mechanic, code, expiration if any>

**Links**: <single shortened destination matching the offer>

**Message Type**: Welcome Offer
```

The brief carries the offer terms and the code verbatim. The copywriter does not invent a percentage or a code.

## Example structural fragment

```
Welcome to <BRAND>, <ONE-LINE POSITIONING FROM BRIEF>. Get <EXACT OFFER> with <EXACT CODE>. [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message tries to fit a brand story, a founder note, and an offer into one SMS, the type has drifted. Trim to welcome plus offer plus link.
