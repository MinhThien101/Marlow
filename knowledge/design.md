# **Ease of Click**

When customers are opening emails they are in zombie mode.

Any friction in the ability for someone to click will automatically lead to churn.

We need to hand the click to them on a silver platter with the following:

* Button above the fold  
    
* Large buttons throughout  
    
* Clear calls to action  
    
* Centered calls to action

# **Button Above the Fold**

The "above the fold" section is the section of the email that is viewed without scrolling.

Not every customer will scroll on your email, so you want a button available in the hero section.

This way EVERY customer has the ability to visit the site and go down the funnel.

Including a top button is an easy low-hanging fruit to automatically improve your click rates.

# **Large Buttons**

An iPhone screen is 2.75 inches.

That's not a large space.

Larger buttons, more surface area, easier ability to click.

This has a massive impact on conversions.

# **Clear Calls To Action**

The goal of an email is to get someone to click.

Your buttons should be the easiest to view part of your email and there should be no ability for someone to click over a button.

Avoid distracting backgrounds and use contrasting colors to get your CTAs to stand out.

# **Centered Buttons**

Most people hold their phone in their right hand.

If you put a button on the left side, the user has to stretch their thumb over to click (more friction).

This actually negatively impacts conversions substantially.

# **Design Skimmability**

Nobody is sitting at their desk annotating their emails.

They're checking emails as they walk to the bathroom, on a quick break, etc

On average you have 2-4 seconds to get your point across.

Optimize for the skim, not for the read.

Include these factors:

* Simplicity  
    
* Clear sections  
    
* Minimal copy  
    
* Clear headlines and main points

# **Simplicity**

Simplicity always wins.

Adding too much complexity in your emails will overwhelm the customer.

More importantly, complex designs will distract from the whole purpose of the email… to sell.

Keep it simple so users can easily skim and understand the contents of the email.

When possible \- shorten your email as much as possible.

# **Clear Sections**

The human brain likes when things are organized and clear.

We want to be smooth with our sections, but clear when we are differentiating.

This way, people can clearly see 2-3 sections, categorize in their head, and skim with ease

The below example makes it clear what is the intro and what is the product section.

# **Minimal Copy**

As mentioned earlier, the customer is only going to be on your email for 3ish seconds.

Nobody is going to be reading an essay in their inbox.

More copy leads to overwhelm, nobody is going to read it, and it's wasted space.

Optimize for the 3 second viewers. Keep it quick and punchy.

# **Headlines and Main Points**

Very few people will actually ready your body copy in full.

But they WILL read / skim your headlines / subheadlines

If we only get 3 seconds of our viewers attention we need to make it loud and clear what our main points are.

# **Design Branding**

A design can be beautiful, but unrelated to a brand.

The strengthen your brand you need congruency across all channels.

Without it, positive associations with the brand from other channels will be lost in email.

This will promote long term conversions.

* Branded graphics  
    
* Correct fonts  
    
* Congruent styling

# **Hero Section Design Mastery**

The hero section is the top part of the email.

It is by far the most important.

Most people will only read the top section so 75% of your efforts should go to this.

# **Optimize Above the Fold**

The "above the fold" section is the section of the email that is viewed without scrolling.

To remove friction for the buyer, we want the above the fold to have everything the customer needs.

A great Above The Fold includes:

* Clear headline  
    
* Strong graphic  
    
* Clear value prop  
    
* Clear button

# **Top Button**

As mentioned before, not every customer will scroll on your email, so you want a button available in the hero section.

This way EVERY customer has the ability to visit the site and go down the funnel.

Including a top button is an easy low-hanging fruit to automatically improve your click rates.

# **Bridge Section Design Mastery**

Your bridge section is the supporting section to your Hero.

It will also serve as a transition into a specific product section if you include one.

In some cases you won't have a bridge, in some you'll have two.

# **Use Sub Headlines and Callouts**

In order for people to be able to skim your email, your bridge should have sub headlines.

Make your points easily visible and trackable in just a few seconds.

# **Different Kinds of Bridges**

The bridge can be anything…

* Supporting copy  
    
* Supporting graphic  
    
* Testimonial(s)  
    
* Photo(s)  
    
* Feature(s)  
    
* Steps / Instructions  
    
* Tips

# **Product Section Design Mastery**

Your product section is exactly as it sounds.

It's the part of the email that is going to specifically feature and highlight a product or products.

Products can even be categories, not necessarily specific products.

# **Individual Calls To Action**

It's important if your showing products to make it clear that the customer can click it and go to that product page.

This removes friction for the buyer so they don't have to go to the general site and find the product.

Make it clear by giving individual buttons or underlines product titles.

# **End With A General CTA**

After you give individual products, it's important to always have a general CTA below.

Maybe the customer is interested, but didn't like any of the products in the email.

By leaving out a general CTA below, you lose out on all these customers.

We've noticed 25% boosts in clicks by doing this.
