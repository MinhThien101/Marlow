# Quiz / Interactive Section

Quiz / Interactive pulls the reader into a quick choice or short quiz that turns engagement into a single next click. Use it when the brand has a real quiz, shade finder, sizing tool, or fit recommender to send the reader into. Use it as a soft, lower-pressure entry point for cold or lukewarm readers (browse abandon, welcome flow mid-sequence, education-led sends). Do not use it when no actual quiz or interactive flow exists on-site to land in; the section is not a stand-in for a CTA when no quiz exists. Counts as a HEAVY section because the question structure takes word count, even though each line is short.

## Structural rules

- The interaction must be immediately obvious. The reader sees "question 1" or "find my shade" and knows what is happening.
- Two valid shapes:
  - **In-email choice block:** 1 to 3 questions in the email, each with 3 to 4 short choice lines (A / B / C). Each question is one line. Each choice is one line. End with ONE CTA into the full quiz / result page.
  - **Quiz launch block:** a single framing question or hook line, plus a one-line description of the quiz outcome, plus ONE CTA into the quiz page.
- Choices are short. One short clause per option. Long choices break the scan.
- Required: one CTA at the bottom pointing at the quiz, poll, or result page. Even when the email shows in-email questions, the real next step is the on-site quiz.
- No invented quiz. The quiz, finder, or interactive flow must exist on the brand's site.

## Placeholder shape in the brief

```
**Email Sections**:
1. Hero Section
2. Quiz / Interactive
3. <next canonical section label>

**Product Focus**: <the product line, collection, or category the quiz routes the reader into>
**Links**: <verified URL of the quiz, shade finder, or interactive page>
```

The brief names the section and the destination quiz link. The questions and choices come from brand state (the actual quiz on the brand's site), not from imagination.

## Example structural fragments

In-email choice block:

```
## <Q1 PROMPT: one line, ends with a colon>

A) <SHORT CHOICE: one clause>

B) <SHORT CHOICE: one clause>

C) <SHORT CHOICE: one clause>

## <Q2 PROMPT: one line, ends with a colon>

A) <SHORT CHOICE>

B) <SHORT CHOICE>

C) <SHORT CHOICE>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: take the full quiz, find my match>](<verified quiz link from brief>)
```

Quiz launch block:

```
## <HOOK HEADING: 2-5 words framing the question the quiz answers>

<ONE LINE: what the quiz does and how long it takes, in plain language>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE: find my shade, take the 30-second quiz>](<verified quiz link from brief>)
```

Pick one shape per send. Do not stack both in the same section.
