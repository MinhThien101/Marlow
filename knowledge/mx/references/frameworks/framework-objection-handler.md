# Framework: Objection Handler

Objection Handler resolves the 3 to 5 specific doubts blocking a warm reader from buying. Use it as the standard text slot in browse-abandon and cart-abandon flows (usually Email 2), and as the second beat after Problem-Solution or Educational Guide sends. Use it when the brand has concrete specifics: dimensions, sourcing, guarantee terms, compatibility specs, return policy. Do not use it without specifics; vague answers ("best quality") sound defensive. Do not use it as a generic FAQ dump. Playbook rule: the objection IS the main tension. Specifics dissolve doubt, not adjectives. Address 3 to 5 objections inline; fewer than 3 is not this framework, more than 5 reads like a help-center page.

## Structural rules

- Opening: name the hesitation directly. One short paragraph. "I get the hesitation" / "You are probably wondering" / "Here is what most people ask" all work.
- Objection list: 3 to 5 specific objections. Each one named, each one answered. Bold the objection or use a clear visual break.
- Specifics: each answer carries a concrete detail (number, dimension, policy term, real spec). Adjectives without specifics fail the framework.
- Reassurance close: one short beat. The low-risk path forward (guarantee, return policy, support reply).
- CTA: one. Destination is the product, the cart, or a specs/FAQ page that backs the answers up. Verb matches ("See the specs", "Try it risk-free", "Return to cart").
- Sign-off: optional. Founder voice common.
- Do not invent a guarantee term, a return window, a dimension, or a compatibility number.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the 3-5 objections the email resolves>

**Product Focus**: <the product the objections are about>

**Links**: <verified destination for proof, often a specs or guarantee page>

**Framework**: Objection Handler
```

The brief lists required objections. Unknown specifics become `missing info: <what is missing>` in the brief.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: name the hesitation directly, one short paragraph>

<OPTIONAL CONTEXT: one or two short lines acknowledging why the doubt is reasonable>

- **<OBJECTION 1: real reader doubt>** <SHORT ANSWER with one concrete specific>
- **<OBJECTION 2>** <SHORT ANSWER with one concrete specific>
- **<OBJECTION 3>** <SHORT ANSWER with one concrete specific>

<REASSURANCE CLOSE: low-risk path forward, one short beat>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If answers read as "highest quality" or "trust us," the framework has drifted into defense. Replace with a concrete spec, policy term, or dimension.
