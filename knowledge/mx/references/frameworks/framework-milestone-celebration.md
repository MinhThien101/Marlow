# Framework: Milestone Celebration

Milestone Celebration recognizes the customer for a real achievement: order count, subscription anniversary, spend threshold, loyalty-program tier, multi-year customer status. Use it inside loyalty flows when a customer crosses a meaningful threshold, and in standalone campaigns when the brand can recognize progress in a real way. Use it when the celebration is earned and verifiable. Do not use it when the milestone is invented (no "happy 6-month-and-2-day anniversary" sends). Playbook rule: recognition is specific and earned. Celebration FIRST, promotion second. Best when the brand can actually reward or acknowledge progress, not just label a customer "valued".

## Structural rules

- Opening: short. Acknowledge the milestone directly with the specific number. "You have placed [COUNT] orders" / "It has been [YEARS] years with us" / "You have crossed [THRESHOLD] in support."
- Why it matters or what it says: one short paragraph. Tie the milestone to a real meaning: the brand's mission, the customer's pattern, the community.
- Small reward, next step, or invitation: one short beat if relevant. A bulk-pricing page, an early-access invitation, a thank-you gift, a VIP threshold note. Skip if the celebration is sentiment-only.
- CTA: one. Soft when sentiment-led, direct when a reward is the lever. Verb matches ("Shop bulk", "Claim my gift", "See what is new for [TIER]").
- Sign-off: personal. Founder first name common.
- Do not lead with the offer. Celebration first, promotion second.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the specific milestone and the recognition>

**Required Language**: <exact threshold count, year number, or tier name>

**Offer**: <reward mechanic if relevant, else "No offer">

**Links**: <verified destination if a CTA is included>

**Framework**: Milestone Celebration
```

The brief carries the verified threshold. The copywriter does not invent a count, a year, or a tier.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

<OPENING: acknowledge the specific milestone with the real number>

<WHY IT MATTERS: one short paragraph tying the milestone to real meaning>

<OPTIONAL REWARD OR INVITATION: one short beat if relevant>

[<OPTIONAL CTA>](<verified link from brief>)

<WARM CLOSE: founder first name>

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the email leads with "Get 20% off!" before the celebration lands, the framework has drifted. Celebration first; the offer or reward sits in service of the recognition, not the other way around.
