# SMS: Bundle Deal

Bundle Deal is the SMS for promoting a packaged set: a multi-product kit, a build-your-own bundle, a curated collection sold as one unit. Use it when the bundle itself is the offer (a complete kit, a paired set, a coverage promise) and the click goes to the bundle PDP or builder. Use it when the value math is obvious enough to land in one sentence. Do not use it for single-product sales dressed up as bundles, and do not use it when the bundle still needs explanation paragraphs to make sense (that belongs in an email). Playbook rule: clarity of value over cleverness. Make the bundle sound complete and useful. Avoid too many product details.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: state the bundle promise in one beat. The job the bundle does, or what it covers. "Every room. One switch." style works.
- Body: name the bundle. Optional one-line value cue (what it includes, who it is for). Skip individual SKU lists.
- CTA: one. Verb tied to the bundle ("Shop the bundle", "See what's inside", "Get the set"). One destination.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the bundle and its primary value claim>

**Product Focus**: <the bundle name, the canonical value angle>

**Offer**: <bundle pricing or discount terms, or "Bundle pricing is the offer">

**Links**: <single shortened bundle URL>

**Message Type**: Bundle Deal
```

The brief carries the bundle name and the value claim verbatim. The copywriter does not invent included SKUs, prices, or savings math.

## Example structural fragment

```
<VALUE PROMISE>. The <BUNDLE_NAME> <ONE-LINE COVERAGE CUE>. [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message tries to list every product in the bundle, the type has drifted. Cut to the promise and the bundle name.
