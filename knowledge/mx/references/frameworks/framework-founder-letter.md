# Framework: Founder Letter

Founder Letter is the brand's most intimate text format. Use it when the brand's voice IS the founder and the send needs a real human signal: post-purchase day-21 check-in, welcome-flow Email 5 with extended discount, VIP-flow sentiment touch, holiday thank-you, year-end reflection, founder-voice winback. Use it when sentiment and trust are the lever, not conversion mechanics. Do not use it when the offer is the campaign's reason to exist (that is Discount Push). Do not use it as a disguised hard sell. Playbook rule: write like a founder talking, not a brand play-acting intimacy. Specific personal observations beat generic gratitude. The product tie-in is natural, never bolted on. The P.S. cross-sell is where post-purchase founder letters make real money; treat it as a structural slot, not an accessory.

## Structural rules

- Greeting: personal. "Hey [FIRST_NAME]," is the default. No "Dear valued customer."
- Founder name early or at sign-off: pick one. Most letters open with "It's [FOUNDER_NAME]" or close with the name and brand. Not both top and bottom.
- Body opening: one specific personal observation, anecdote, or honest reflection. Not "I just wanted to reach out." Concrete beats vague.
- Body middle: what the moment, observation, or season means. Short. One or two beats.
- Product or offer tie-in: natural. Connect the reflection to the product, the season, the milestone, or the offer. If the tie-in feels bolted on, the letter has drifted.
- CTA: one inline link or one button-style line. Verb matches the moment ("Stock up", "Shop the collection", "Use my code"). Soft CTAs are allowed when the letter is sentiment-only.
- Sign-off: real. Founder's first name, optional brand name, optional honest closer ("Best", "Long live boyhood", "With smiles"). No corporate close.
- P.S. cross-sell: standard structural slot in post-purchase founder letters. Optional but high-leverage. One product, one link, one line of context.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the moment the founder is speaking into>

**Product Focus**: <the product the tie-in points at, or "No product tie-in" for sentiment-only sends>

**Offer**: <exact mechanic if any, or "No offer">

**Framework**: Founder Letter
```

The brief does not pre-write the founder's voice. The copywriter calibrates from the brand profile, memories, and recent founder-voice examples.

## Example structural fragment

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME],

It's [FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME].

<SPECIFIC PERSONAL OBSERVATION: one concrete beat, not generic gratitude>

<WHAT IT MEANS: one or two short lines tying the observation to the brand or the season>

<PRODUCT OR OFFER TIE-IN: natural, not bolted on>

[<CTA VERB PHRASE>](<verified link from brief>)

<OPTIONAL CLOSER>

[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME] @ [BRAND]

<OPTIONAL P.S. CROSS-SELL: one product, one link, one line>

[UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK]
```

If the opening reads like a brand newsletter ("We're so excited to share..."), the framework has drifted. Rewrite from the founder's actual voice, not a brand voice imitating one.
