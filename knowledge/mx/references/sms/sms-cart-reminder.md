# SMS: Cart Reminder

Cart Reminder is the SMS for someone who added items and stopped short of checkout. Use it inside cart-abandon SMS sequences, or as a one-off nudge when the platform allows transactional cart SMS. Use it when the reader is close to buying and the lever is friction, not persuasion: they were there, the cart still exists, the path back is one tap. Do not use it for browse abandon where intent is lower, do not use it for cold list-wide sends, and do not use it as a disguised promo. Playbook rule: write to someone close to buying. Helpful and direct beats clever. One recovery CTA.

## Structural rules

- Length: under 160 characters total, including link.
- Opening: short. One beat acknowledging the cart. "Looks like checkout didn't get finished" reads better than "Forgetting something?" which is forbidden in MX cart copy across channels.
- Body: optional one-line product cue when the brief carries item specifics. Generic "your cart" is acceptable when the brief does not name items.
- CTA: one. Verb pointing back to the CART, not the product page, not the homepage. "Complete your order", "Pick up where you left off", "Finish checkout" all fit.
- Sign-off: none.
- Compliance: links shortened; STOP handled by the platform.

## When the brief should call it

```
**Campaign Direction**: <one sentence naming the cart recovery angle>

**Offer**: <welcome discount terms or "No offer for past buyers">

**Links**: <shortened cart return URL>

**Message Type**: Cart Reminder
```

The brief names the lever and the cart URL. The copywriter does not invent items, quantities, or offer terms.

## Example structural fragment

```
Looks like checkout didn't get finished. <OPTIONAL CART CUE>. <CTA VERB>: [SHORT_LINK]
```

If the message starts negotiating or apologizing for sending the reminder, the type has drifted. Trim to: cart exists, here is the path back.
